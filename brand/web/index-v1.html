II Process

From phone call to launched in five days.

Most agencies bill like law firms — quarters, retainers, change orders. We work like a kitchen — order at lunch, ready by dinner. Here's a typical week.

  1. Monday

    You talk.

    A 30-minute call. You tell us what you sell, what you want, and who you're trying to reach. No questionnaire, no homework. We take notes.

  2. Tuesday

    We sketch.

    By end of day, you get a one-page scope: what's in, what's out, what we'll deliver, and a flat price. No tiered packages. No "starting at."

  3. Wednesday – Thursday

    We build.

    Our human team — using our AI production engine — designs, writes, and ships. You get a private preview link every evening. No surprises on Friday.

  4. Friday

    You launch.

    One short review call, a few small tweaks, and we ship it live — usually before the weekend. We stick around for two weeks of free small edits, no questions asked.

  5. Always

    A real human, by name.

    One person — not a ticket queue — handles your account. You'll have their cell number. They live here.

IV About

A local agency, with superpowers.

Marketing got broken in two directions at once.

On one side, traditional agencies bill like law firms — six-figure retainers, six-month timelines, and a CMS the client is too afraid to log into. On the other side, "DIY AI" tells small business owners to learn prompting, learn Squarespace, learn analytics, and learn to be their own creative director on the side of running their actual business.

Human AI Marketing exists for the middle. The future of marketing isn't humans replaced by AI or humans drowning in AI — it's humans amplified by AI, on behalf of other humans. Our local interns are the face. Our AI toolkit is the engine. Our clients are the reason.
