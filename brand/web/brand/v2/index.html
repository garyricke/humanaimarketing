mission

teach young people to build audiences with AI — while staying recognizably human.

place them with the local businesses, churches, schools, and civic groups that need this help. the work that comes out of that exchange is the product. the agency is the trellis.

philosophy

two ideas anchor the brand.

初心

Shoshin — beginner's mind.

approach every project as if for the first time. curiosity over expertise. questions over answers. the intern's first day is the agency's default posture.

侘寂

Wabi-Sabi — beauty in imperfection.

asymmetry. weathering. the visible hand of the maker. we will not airbrush our work into hollowness. the smudge and the off-kilter line are evidence we were here.

what we do

a mentorship-led practice, in three flows.

  1. i.

    train.

    a rolling cohort of high-school and college students learn AI-assisted audience building inside our handbook and weekly sessions. apprenticeship rhythms, not bootcamp gloss.

  2. ii.

    place.

    we pair each apprentice with a real local engagement — a parish bulletin, a chamber email, a shop's website. they do the work; a manager reviews.

  3. iii.

    connect.

    every engagement is also a tie between two parts of the city — an 18-year-old learning the craft, a 60-year-old shopkeeper learning the tools. the byproduct is community, not just deliverables.

what this brand is not

a quiet list, by negation.

not a tech brand. no glow, no gradients, no abstract data-flow illustrations.

not a hype brand. we do not announce. we do the work and let people find it.

not a polished corporate brand. the slightly crooked line stays in.

not an AI brand. AI is in our toolbox, not our mission statement.
