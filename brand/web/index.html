i · philosophy

two ideas anchor the work.

we use the fastest tools in the world. we also slow down enough to notice what they cannot do. the brand sits on that tension.

初心

Shoshin — beginner's mind.

approach every project as if for the first time. curiosity over expertise. questions over answers. the intern's first day is the agency's default posture.

侘寂

Wabi-Sabi — beauty in imperfection.

asymmetry. weathering. the visible hand of the maker. we will not airbrush our work into hollowness. the smudge and the off-kilter line are evidence we were here.

ii · what we do

a mentorship-led practice, in three flows.

we train. we place. we connect. each cycle ties a young person learning a new craft to a local business that needs the help — and puts a little more thread between two parts of the city.

i.

train.

a rolling cohort of high-school and college students learn the AI toolkit through our handbook and weekly sessions. apprenticeship rhythms, not bootcamp gloss.

  • 16–22 year olds from the tri-cities
  • real briefs, real reviews, real bylines
  • paid hours; public credit

ii.

place.

we pair each apprentice with a real local engagement — a parish bulletin, a chamber email, a shop's website. they do the work. a manager reviews.

  • one apprentice, one local partner
  • scope sized to a season
  • review by a senior team member

iii.

connect.

every engagement is also a thread between two parts of the city. an 18-year-old learning the craft. a 60-year-old learning the tools. the byproduct is community.

  • local first, always
  • credit visible on every deliverable
  • graduates stay in the network

the door, on two sides.
