# Human AI Marketing — Brand Guide

*Version 1.0 — Established 2026 · Batavia, Illinois*

---

## 1. Brand Foundation

### 1.1 Mission
We make world-class marketing accessible to local businesses by pairing **generative AI speed** with **human white-glove service**. Our clients never touch code, prompts, or dashboards — they hand off their goals, and our human team executes at the speed of AI.

### 1.2 Vision
A tri-city area (Batavia, Geneva, St. Charles) — and eventually all of Chicagoland — where every local business, church, school, and civic organization has a digital presence as polished as a Fortune 500's, at a fraction of the cost and time.

### 1.3 Brand Promise
**"You talk. We build. In days, not quarters."**

A local business should be able to call us on Monday and see new pages, new campaigns, or new creative live by Friday — delivered by a real person they know by name.

### 1.4 Positioning Statement
For local business owners and regional employers who feel priced out or talked-over by traditional agencies, **Human AI Marketing** is the only agency in the Fox Valley that pairs a dedicated human manager with a full-stack AI production engine — so you get agency-grade work at intern-friendly turnaround times, without ever having to learn the tools yourself.

---

## 2. Brand Story

Marketing got broken in two directions at once.

On one side, traditional agencies bill like law firms — six-figure retainers, six-month timelines, and a CMS the client is too afraid to log into. On the other side, "DIY AI" tells small business owners to learn prompting, learn Squarespace, learn analytics, and learn to be their own creative director on the side of running their actual business.

Human AI Marketing exists for the middle. We believe the future of marketing isn't *humans replaced by AI* or *humans drowning in AI* — it's **humans amplified by AI, on behalf of other humans**. Our local interns are the face. Our AI toolkit is the engine. Our clients are the reason.

We are unapologetically local. We start in our own backyard, learn the names of the shops on Wilson Street and Third Street, and grow from there.

## 4. Brand Personality

If Human AI Marketing were a person, they would be:

- **A trusted neighbor** who happens to have a computer science degree
- **A confident generalist**, not a jargon-heavy specialist
- **Warm but precise** — friendly in conversation, exacting in delivery

**We are:** approachable, fast, capable, local, modern, candid
**We are not:** cold, corporate, hype-driven, jargon-heavy, gatekeeping, AI-evangelizing

## 5. Voice & Tone

### 5.1 Voice (consistent across all channels)
- **Plainspoken.** No buzzwords. "Website" not "digital experience." "Email" not "omnichannel touchpoint."
- **Confident, not boastful.** State what we deliver. Skip the hype.
- **Specific.** Name streets, names, businesses, timeframes. Vague is the opposite of local.
- **Warm.** Contractions are welcome. So is the occasional dry joke.

### 5.2 Tone (shifts by context)

| Context | Tone |
| :---- | :---- |
| Homepage / sales | Confident, inviting, slightly bold |
| Client emails | Warm, concise, action-oriented |
| Intern-authored content | Curious, energetic, learning-out-loud |
| Proposals / SOWs | Crisp, transparent, numbers-forward |
| Social posts | Conversational, hyper-local, image-led |

### 5.3 Voice Examples

**✅ On-brand**
> "We rebuilt the Batavia Park District's events page in four days. Same content, half the load time, zero CMS logins for their staff."

**❌ Off-brand**
> "Leveraging cutting-edge generative AI, we orchestrated a holistic digital transformation for a key municipal stakeholder."

**✅ On-brand**
> "Hi Tad — your new homepage is live. Two small things I'd like your eyes on before we send the newsletter Friday."

**❌ Off-brand**
> "Per our previous correspondence, please find attached the deliverables for your review at your earliest convenience."

### 5.4 Words We Use / Avoid

| Use | Avoid |
| :---- | :---- |
| build, ship, launch | leverage, synergize, ideate |
| local, neighborhood, hometown | hyperlocal-first ecosystem |
| AI tools, AI workflow | AI-powered, AI-driven, AI-enabled (overused) |
| website, page, email | digital asset, touchpoint |
| client, business owner | stakeholder, end-user |
| intern, manager, team | resource, headcount |

## 6. Target Audiences

### 6.1 Primary: The Local Business Owner
- Owns 1–3 storefronts, a service business, or a regional B2B
- Spends $0–$2,000/month on marketing today, mostly inconsistently
- Doesn't want to learn Squarespace, doesn't want to "approve copy" by email chain
- Trusts referrals, sponsorships, and people they see at the chamber lunch
- **Need we solve:** marketing that looks expensive, costs reasonable, and requires zero effort from them

### 6.2 Secondary: The Mid-Market / Corporate Account ("Uncle Tad" tier)
- $5M–$50M revenue, often family- or founder-owned
- Has been quoted $80K–$250K by traditional agencies
- Skeptical of agencies but knows their site is dated
- **Need we solve:** elite-tier deliverables, transparent pricing, faster than incumbents

### 6.3 Tertiary: Civic & Community Organizations
- Parks, schools, churches, chambers of commerce, libraries
- Often have volunteer-run websites and zero budget
- Live inside our hyper-local AI persona hubs (BataviaIL.co, etc.)
- **Need we solve:** free or sponsored visibility inside our community portals, paid premium upgrades

### 6.4 Internal Audience: The Intern
- College or late-high-school student in the tri-cities
- Curious about tech, marketing, design, or business
- **What we promise:** real client exposure, AI-toolkit fluency, public credit, paid hours

## 8. Visual Identity

### 8.1 Logo Concept

**Wordmark structure:** `human` · `AI` · `marketing`

- "human" and "marketing" set in a warm humanist sans-serif, lowercase
- "AI" set in a clean geometric monospace or technical sans, uppercase — visually distinct, like a circuit between two human elements
- Optional dot or pulse separator (`·`) between each word to emphasize the *handoff* between human and machine

**Mark / favicon:** A circular monogram containing a stylized **`h·AI`** or two overlapping arcs (one warm, one cool) forming a small "infinity" shape — the human-AI loop.

**Lockup rules:**
- Always preserve minimum clear-space of one "h" cap-height on all sides
- Never set the wordmark on a busy photo background without a solid plate or scrim
- Never alter the case (always `human AI marketing`, never `HUMAN AI MARKETING` or `Human Ai Marketing`)

### 8.2 Color Palette

A two-temperature system: **warm humans, cool intelligence, grounded in Midwestern neutrals.**

**Primary**

| Name | Hex | Usage |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Ink Navy | `#0F1B2D` | Primary text, dark UI surfaces, wordmark default |
| Bone | `#F6F1E7` | Primary background, page canvas, warm white |

**Accent (the "human / AI" pair)**

| Name | Hex | Usage |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Hearth Orange | `#E2733B` | Human / warmth — CTAs, highlights, intern-facing brand |
| Signal Cyan | `#3DBDC9` | AI / intelligence — tech badges, AI-powered indicators, data viz |

**Supporting neutrals**

| Name | Hex | Usage |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Limestone | `#C9C2B3` | Dividers, secondary UI, muted panels |
| Slate | `#4A5663` | Body copy on light, secondary text |
| Pure White | `#FFFFFF` | Stark contrast, photo overlays |

**Usage rules**
- Hearth Orange and Signal Cyan should rarely appear on the same surface in equal weight — one leads, one accents
- Avoid pure black; use Ink Navy instead
- Bone is the default background; pure white is reserved for high-contrast moments
- Maintain WCAG AA contrast minimum (4.5:1 for body text)

### 8.3 Typography

**Headline:** *A humanist serif or warm display sans* — e.g., **Fraunces**, **Söhne Breit**, or **GT Sectra**. Choose one with a slight handcraft feel; we are not a tech startup.

**Body:** *A neutral, highly readable sans-serif* — e.g., **Inter**, **Söhne**, or **Geist**. Set at 16–18px minimum on web.

**Code / "AI" mark:** *A geometric monospace* — e.g., **JetBrains Mono** or **Berkeley Mono**. Reserved for the "AI" in the wordmark, technical UI, and code-adjacent contexts.

**Hierarchy guidelines**
- Headlines: tight tracking, generous line-height (1.1–1.2), sentence case
- Body: open tracking, line-height 1.5–1.65
- Avoid ALL-CAPS except for short labels (≤ 4 words) in supporting roles

### 8.4 Imagery & Photography

**Subject matter (in priority order):**
1. **Real local places** — recognizable tri-cities storefronts, streets, parks, the Fox River
2. **Real clients and interns at work** — natural light, candid, never stock-staged
3. **Tactile process shots** — sketches, screens mid-build, hands on a keyboard
4. **Negative space and architecture** — Midwestern light, brick, stone, water

**Treatment:**
- Warm, slightly desaturated color grade — never cold or clinical
- Honest light — daylight, golden hour, interior tungsten preferred over flash
- Wide and medium shots over tight close-ups
- Avoid: stock photography, AI-generated humans, "futuristic blue glow" cyber-aesthetics, gear-and-circuit clichés

### 8.5 Iconography & UI

- **Style:** Two-stroke line icons with rounded terminals. Pair with the occasional duotone fill in Hearth Orange + Signal Cyan to signal "human + AI."
- **Weight:** Consistent 1.5–2px stroke at 24px base
- **Corners:** Slightly rounded (2–4px) on UI elements — soft, not pillowy
- **Motion:** Quick, confident easings (200–300ms). Nothing bouncy or playful — we are fast, not frivolous.

### 8.6 Layout Principles

- Generous margins; the page should breathe
- Left-aligned text dominates; centered text reserved for hero moments only
- One bold element per screen — avoid competing focal points
- "Local" details (street names, neighborhood photos, intern bylines) live in visible corners, not buried in footers

## 9. Applications

### 9.1 Website
- Static front-end (HTML / CSS / JS) deployed via Netlify
- Cloudinary for all images and video
- Page weight target: < 200KB above the fold; Lighthouse score 95+
- Every page surfaces (a) a named human contact, (b) a turnaround estimate, (c) a tri-cities reference

### 9.2 Email
- Plain-text-leaning HTML; minimal imagery
- Always signed by a named human (intern or manager), not "The Team"
- Subject lines: declarative, lowercase, under 50 characters

### 9.3 Proposals & SOWs
- Single-page summary first; details after
- Pricing shown as: *AI compute estimate · human hours · total* — transparency is a feature
- Always include a stated turnaround in business days

### 9.4 Hyper-Local Persona Hubs (BataviaIL.co et al.)
- Co-branded "Powered by Human AI Marketing" footer mark in Signal Cyan
- Persona pages adopt local civic color cues but stay anchored to our type system
- Premium business profiles get the Hearth Orange "verified neighbor" badge

### 9.5 Intern-Facing Materials
- Branded onboarding kit, named credit on every deliverable they touch
- "Built by [Intern Name], Human AI Marketing" byline standard on case studies and portal pages

## 10. What This Brand Is Not

To stay honest, here's what we explicitly are **not**:

- **Not an AI consultancy.** We use AI; we don't sell AI literacy to clients.
- **Not a SaaS.** No platform login. No subscription dashboard. Humans deliver work.
- **Not a national agency.** We grow neighborhood by neighborhood.
- **Not a discount shop.** We're faster and leaner, but our work is premium-tier.
- **Not anti-human.** Despite the name, AI is the *engine*, not the *brand*. Humans are the brand.
