*Exploratory. Paper-first. Quiet, imperfect, beginner's mind.*

## 1. Foundation

### Mission
Teach young people in the Fox Valley to build audiences and grow communities using AI — while staying recognizably human. Place them with the local businesses, churches, schools, and civic groups that need this help. The work that comes out of that exchange is the product. The agency is the trellis.

### Vision
A more vibrant city, made one local partnership at a time — apprentices and shop owners working in the same room, learning the same tools, sharing the same neighborhood.

### North Star
**Beginner's mind. Imperfect hand. Local roots.**

---

## 2. Philosophy

Two ideas anchor the brand.

**Shoshin · 初心 — beginner's mind.**
Approach every project, every client, every tool, as if for the first time. Curiosity over expertise. Questions over answers. The intern's first day is the agency's default posture.

**Wabi-Sabi · 侘寂 — beauty in imperfection.**
Asymmetry, weathering, the visible hand of the maker. We will not airbrush our work into hollowness. The smudge, the off-kilter line, the human pause — these are evidence we were here.

Together they form a posture: humble, attentive, unhurried, honest. We use the fastest tools in the world, but we slow down enough to notice what they cannot do.

---

## 3. What We Actually Do

A mentorship-led practice in three flows.

1. **Train.** A rolling cohort of high-school and college students learn AI-assisted audience building inside our handbook and weekly sessions. Apprenticeship rhythms, not bootcamp gloss.

2. **Place.** We pair each apprentice with a real local engagement: a parish bulletin, a chamber email, a shop's website. They do the work; a manager reviews.

3. **Connect.** Every engagement is also a tie between two parts of the city — an 18-year-old learning the craft, a 60-year-old shopkeeper learning the tools. The byproduct is community, not just deliverables.

## 5. Voice

Short. Direct. Lowercase by default.

Lean declarative. Trust the reader. Resist adjectives.

Avoid technical jargon, marketing jargon, and AI evangelism in equal measure. Speak the way a careful person speaks at the kitchen table.

### Voice in practice

| ✅ | ❌ |
| :--- | :--- |
| we teach interns to build audiences with AI. | We leverage cutting-edge generative tools to empower digital-native talent. |
| your newsletter is live. two small notes. | Per our prior correspondence, please find attached the deliverables for your review. |
| this is a beginner's draft. that is the point. | Our seasoned team of experts has produced... |
| three apprentices. one shop. one summer. | Strategic engagement across multiple verticals. |

Headlines: five words or fewer when possible. Body copy: ~14 words per sentence. Read it aloud — if it sounds like a press release, rewrite.

## 6. Audiences

**Primary — Apprentices.**
Tri-cities students, roughly 16–22, curious about AI, design, writing, or business. They are not labor. They are the program.

**Secondary — Local hosts.**
Shops, churches, schools, civic groups in Batavia, Geneva, St. Charles. They host an apprentice and a small project. The work is real; the price is right.

## 7. Visual Identity

### 7.1 Logo

A hand-drawn circle. Inside it, a single tilde-like stroke — a wave, a breath, a smile. Below the mark, the wordmark `h·AI` in a humanist serif: `h` and the dot in ink; `AI` in muted teal.

The circle is **not** perfectly round. The wave is **not** perfectly centered. That is the brand.

**Three lockups:**
- *Mark only* — favicons, social avatars, stamps
- *Mark + wordmark* (vertical, centered) — primary lockup
- *Wordmark only* — inline use, footers, signatures

Always preserve clear space equal to one circle-height on every side.

### 7.2 Color

A small palette. Warm paper, ink, one quiet teal. That's it.

| Name | Hex | Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Paper | `#EDE4D2` | The warmer of the two off-whites. Default background, *or* the differentiated/lift surface when Bone is the base. |
| Bone | `#F7EFDD` | The lighter of the two off-whites. Lifted surface above Paper, *or* the default base when a page wants to feel airier. |
| Ink | `#1B2738` | Primary text, hand-drawn lines, logo strokes. |
| Teal | `#4A7682` | Reserved for the `AI` in the wordmark and rare accents. |
| Sumi | `#0F1620` | Deepest tone, used sparingly for emphasis or footers. |

**Base / lift is a per-page decision.** Either Paper-as-base with Bone lifts, or Bone-as-base with Paper lifts. Pick one per page and hold it; never alternate within a single document.

- *Paper-base* (warmer, denser) suits long-read content like the handbook, articles, and the muted hero panel sequence.
- *Bone-base* (lighter, airier) suits marketing surfaces where photography is doing the work — image-led carousels, hero overlays, gallery layouts.

The v2 preview page demonstrates the Bone-base variant. The handbook will stay Paper-base.

No orange. No bright cyan. No gradients. No drop shadows.

When in doubt: more of the base color, less of everything else.

### 7.3 Typography

**Headline:** A humanist serif with quiet personality.
*Default:* **Fraunces**, variable axis, optical-size 9–144, weight range 300–800.

**Body:** A clean grotesque sans.
*Default:* **Inter** at 16–17px, line-height 1.6, weight 400.

**Hand:** A single hand-drawn font for accents, signatures, and the occasional pull-quote. Default: **Caveat** (or a scan of the founder's hand). Never used for body.

#### Weight scale — confident, not delicate

Quiet voice, *thick type.* Headlines over photography or in display sizes carry real weight; the minimalism lives in layout and content, not in stroke thickness. Apple proved this — display heads run 600–700 even on quiet pages.

| Role | Weight | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Hero title (over image) | **700** | Tight tracking (-0.025em). The brand's most confident moment. |
| Sub-headers (flow / path / panel titles) | **600** | On Paper next to or below images. Tight tracking (-0.012 to -0.015em). |
| Section titles | **500–600** | On Bone. 600 when the section needs presence, 500 when it's a quiet intro. |
| Article titles | **500** | Smaller scale, still confident. |
| Wordmark | **400** | The logo stays light — it's a brand mark, not a headline. Elegance reads. |
| Body lede (serif) | **500** | Substantial but not boldface. |
| Body copy (sans) | **400** | Default reading weight. |
| Italic accent words | matches surrounding | Italic is a *voice* mark, not a weight mark — keep weight consistent with the line. |

**Tracking guidelines**
- The heavier the weight, the tighter the tracking. Display weights at 700 want -0.022 to -0.028em.
- Section weights at 500–600 want -0.008 to -0.014em.
- Body keeps slightly positive tracking (~0.005em) for reading ease.

**Color guidelines**
- Italic teal accents at body sizes: `--teal` (#4A7682).
- Italic teal accents at display sizes (hero, flow, path): `--teal-deep` (#355864) — the extra contrast is required at large sizes over busy backgrounds.

**Hierarchy basics**
- Lowercase or sentence case. Never ALL-CAPS.
- Avoid bold *for emphasis within a paragraph* — use italic. But headlines themselves should be bold by default.
- Line-height tightens as weight increases: 1.08–1.12 for sub-heads, 1.02–1.05 for hero display.

### 7.4 Imagery

Photography is the most beautiful thing on the page. The interface gets out of the way.

- *Subject:* real people in real rooms; real paper on real desks; real Fox Valley light
- *Treatment:* minimal post-processing; slightly warm white-balance; occasional film grain
- *Composition:* asymmetry welcome; breathing room mandatory
- *Avoid:* stock photography; AI-rendered humans; futuristic gradients; tech-aesthetic glow

### 7.5 Hand-Drawn Elements

A recurring motif. A line under a headline. A circle around a key word. A small arrow pointing to a CTA. These are scanned ink marks, not vector approximations.

Imperfection is the signature.

### 7.6 Layout

- Generous margins; assume the page is paper.
- One idea per screen.
- Left-align body; center only for solitary headlines or marks.
- Whitespace is content.

### 7.7 Hero Panel System

Marketing pages alternate between two panel types — full-bleed photography and quiet typographic statements — at the same scale. Think Apple, but on paper.

**Five repeatable panel types.** Compose pages by sequencing them. Most pages need 4–6 panels.

| Panel | Purpose | Typical contents |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Photo, full-bleed** | The hero or scene-setter. Image carries the emotion. | 16:9 tri-cities photograph; one short serif headline below; optional one-line caption in the hand font. |
| **Type-only** | A pause. A question or a claim. | One serif sentence ≤8 words; optional hand-drawn underline on a single phrase; bone or paper background. |
| **Quote** | An italic chapter epigraph. | One italic serif statement, ≤14 words, centered, generous padding. |
| **Split** | A grounded moment with context. | Photo on one side, headline + 1-sentence body on the other. Bone background on the copy side. |
| **Closing** | The signature at the bottom of the page. | A single line in the hand font: *begin again.* / *thanks for reading.* / *— Gary.* |

**Rules of the panel system**
- One idea per panel. If you can split it into two, you should.
- Headlines: ≤5 words when possible, lowercase, period at the end.
- Italic + teal carries the accent word in each headline. Never two accent words.
- Hand-drawn underlines are reserved for one phrase per page.
- No photographic panel may be followed by another photographic panel — alternate with type.
- The closing panel is always the last one. It is the brand exhaling.

A reference sequence lives on the v2 preview page at `/brand/v2/`.

### 7.8 Carousel Pattern

For seasonal content, story collections, or anything that needs to sit *side-by-side* rather than top-to-bottom, use a horizontal scroll-snap carousel. Borrowed from Apple's homepage story rails.

**Anatomy:**
- A left-aligned intro block: eyebrow + short headline + 1-paragraph lede
- A pair of round prev/next arrow buttons on the right
- A horizontal track of cards (3:2 image, sentence-case serif headline, one-line body)
- One eyebrow per card in muted teal — usually a thematic tag (e.g., *in season*, *story 03 of 05*)

**Rules:**
- Cards must share aspect ratio (always 3:2 for photographic carousels).
- 4–7 cards is the sweet spot. Fewer feels under-justified; more loses momentum.
- Card headlines stay ≤5 words. Bodies ≤24 words.
- The carousel may use **saturated** photography — it's the one place the brand opens up to bold color, because the paper, serif, and teal frame holds the system together.
- One thematic accent color per carousel is fine (a single hue echoed across cards). Avoid clashing palettes within a single rail.

A reference carousel ("spring 2026") lives on the v2 preview page.

### 7.9 Text-Over-Image Heroes

Some heroes ask the photograph to do almost all the work. Type sits *on* the image, not below it. Three placements cover the cases — pick one per hero and don't mix within a single block.

| Variant | When to use | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bottom scrim** | Busy or mid-tone images where no quadrant is clean. | Linear-gradient scrim from transparent at top to ~78% ink at bottom. Bone-cream type, centered in lower third. The cinematic / film-poster move. |
| **Upper-left ink** | Images with an open, bright quadrant (sky, wall, water) in one corner. | No scrim, or a very faint Bone vignette. Deep-ink type and muted-teal accents — the editorial-magazine move. |
| **Pinned card** | Images where the subject occupies the frame and no quadrant is truly clean. | A small Bone card (~360px wide) pinned to one corner, with rounded edges and a soft shadow. Type sits in the card. The news-magazine cover move. |

**Rules**
- Type on a photograph must clear **4.5:1 contrast** at all sizes. Test before shipping — a bright leaf or window can break legibility.
- Bottom-scrim variant: only Bone-cream type. Never teal italic on the scrim (teal disappears).
- Upper-left and pinned-card variants: ink type, with one italic accent in teal.
- One eyebrow line, one headline (≤6 words), one body line. The image carries everything else.
- Heroes are never stacked tighter than the height of one viewport — they need to breathe.

A reference triptych lives on the v2 preview page (`/brand/v2/`) directly after the muted panel sequence.

## 8. Applications

| Surface | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Website** | Paper background. Single column on mobile, narrow column on desktop. The handbook reads like a book. |
| **Email** | Plain text or near-plain. Signed by a named person. |
| **Print** | Cream stock when possible. Letterpress feel, even when offset. |
| **Social** | Square paper-textured cards. One image, one line of type. Rarely more. |
| **Apprentice materials** | The intern's name and hand are visible on every deliverable they touch. |

## 9. What This Brand Is Not

- **Not a tech brand.** No glow, no gradients, no abstract data-flow illustrations.
- **Not a hype brand.** We do not announce. We do the work and let people find it.
- **Not a polished corporate brand.** The slightly crooked line stays in.
- **Not an AI brand.** AI is in our toolbox, not our mission statement.
- **Not an agency in the traditional sense.** The mentorship is the point. The deliverables are the byproduct.
